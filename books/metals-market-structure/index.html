Metals Market Structure

Metals look like separate markets — until they aren't.
When the regime shifts, they move together. Most participants find out the hard way.

Gold is supposed to be a hedge. Silver follows gold — until it tracks copper instead. Platinum and palladium barely move for months, then gap overnight on a supply event nobody was watching. And during genuine stress, everything declines simultaneously, including the positions you thought were uncorrelated.

The problem isn't the positions. It's the framework. Most participants understand one metal reasonably well and treat the rest as background noise. That works until the dominant driver changes — and when it changes, the relationships that held stop holding, often without warning.

This book builds the structural framework for the full complex: what defines each metal's identity, how liquidity is distributed, how sessions shape price formation, and — most importantly — how the regime determines which relationships hold and which collapse. The mechanics that explain what went wrong are the same ones that let you see it coming next time.

The problem

Why metals frameworks fail under stress

Most metals analysis is built for one regime. The market runs through several.

Gold's real yield relationship holds — until liquidity stress forces selling across everything liquid. Copper tracks Chinese demand — until macro risk-off overwhelms the physical signal. Silver amplifies gold — until its industrial identity pulls it in the opposite direction. Platinum and palladium trade quietly for months — then gap on a supply event in a market too thin to absorb it.

None of this is random. Each failure has a structural explanation. But those explanations require understanding the metals complex as a system — not as five separate instruments that happen to trade on the same exchange.

The reader

This is not an introduction to metals.

It assumes you already trade. It explains what your framework is missing.

If you trade gold, silver, or copper and have been wrong about a move in a way you couldn't fully explain afterward — the explanation is usually structural. A regime shift you didn't see coming. A liquidity condition that changed the rules mid-position. A cross-metal relationship that held for months and then didn't.

This book gives you the language and the mechanics to understand those episodes — and to recognize the conditions that produce them before they produce losses.

Volume I of this series examined currency market structure. Volume II extends the same framework into metals. The two books are independent — but together they build a consistent structural approach across two of the most complex markets available to active participants.

What's not in it

No setups. No signals. No targets.

This is the layer underneath the method — not the method itself.

No trade setups. No entry signals. No chart patterns. No "buy gold when inflation rises" conclusions. No backtest results.

If you want a system, this isn't it. If you want to understand why your system behaves the way it does when conditions change — that's exactly what this is.
